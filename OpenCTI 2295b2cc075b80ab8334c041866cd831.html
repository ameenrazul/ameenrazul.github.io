OpenCTI

OpenCTI¬†is another open-sourced platform designed to provide organisations with the means to manage¬†CTI¬†through the storage, analysis, visualisation and presentation of threat campaigns, malware and IOCs.

OpenCTI uses a variety of knowledge schemas in structuring data, the main one being the Structured Threat Information Expression (STIX2) standards
It allows for the data to be implemented as entities and relationships, effectively tracing the origin of the provided information.

‚ò†Ô∏è Threats Tab

All known threat-related data is grouped here:

  • Threat Actors: Individuals/groups launching attacks.
  • Intrusion Sets:
    • TTPs, tools, malware, and infrastructure used by threat groups.
    • Example: APTs and other persistent threat groups.
  • Campaigns:
    • Coordinated attacks over time by APTs or cybercrime groups.
    • Have specific goals and targets.

üß∞ Arsenal Tab

Lists technical tools used in attacks, both malicious and legitimate:

  • Malware: Active trojans and malware with related intel (e.g., 4H RAT).
  • Attack Patterns:
    • Tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) like Command-Line Interface.
  • Courses of Action (CoA):
    • Defensive measures mapped to MITRE ATT&CK techniques.
  • Tools:
    • Legit tools (e.g., CMD) that may be abused by attackers.
  • Vulnerabilities:
    • Pulled from CVE databases.
    • Used to enrich understanding of exploitable weaknesses.
